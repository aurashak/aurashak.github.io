    • Assignment #2: Map your indigenous area(s)
    Complete by: February 13th


    Step 1: Create the map
    • A. Visit https://geojson.io

    • B. Use the tools to draw a boundary around the areas in the world that you consider indigenous to your family. In the example below, I chose the area of Kabul that my family claims as their origin.
      • There is no hard definition of what "indigenous" means. Use our class discussion, the resources we reviewed in class, readings, and your own online research to define indigeneity for yourself.
      • Consider speaking with your elders, if possible, to find out details.
      • Feel free to choose more than one area
      • Large and/or small territories based on your definition and affiliation.
      • If you do not know or cannot map your indigenous areas, map one of the places you identified in assignment 1. In any case, contemplate how the places you mapped and added to the vision board in the first assignment, relate to the question of (your) indigineity.



    • C.The webpage will produce a list of coordinates in the right column. Copy the coordinates by pushing this button.


    • D. Save the file with the .geojson extension (ex. kabul.geojson).



    • E. Rename the file and move it to the correct location.
