Mapping Resources



                      

Overview

These resources are focused on using "open source" webtools, in that they are publicly accessible and free of charge, to a certain extent. These resources are also focused on being interchangegable and provide easily accessible documentation via chatbots, community forums, and other websites.


Web map platforms


These platforms provide the foundation for maps used on this website. They have connectivity with mapping street map and satellite databases that provide tile imagery.    
  • https://LeafletJS.com is used as a platform for loading Open Street Maps, satellite data, and uploading geojson files.
  • https://Mapbox.com is used as a platform for loading Open Street Maps, satellite data, and uploading geojson files.
  • https://CesiumJS.com, is used as a platform for 3-d view of the earth, including satellite and OSM data.


Web hosting



https://pages.github.com/ is used to host the majority of webfiles associated with these website, aside from the maptiles that are accessed via the web map platforms.


Shapefile and Geojson editing software


https://QGIS.org software was used for editing shapefiles and exporting them to geojson format for upload to Leaflet, Cesium, etc.


APIs (Application Programming Interface)



  • Google Maps API used for access to images tilesets for 3d buildings, and for real time air quality data.
  • Air Quality Programmatic APIs

Shapefile downloads



  • New York City Open Data is used as a platform for loading Open Street Maps, satellite data, and uploading geojson files.
  • https://www.geofabrik.de/data/shapefiles.html, download New Jersey and New York state roads and state boundaries.

Geojson downloads


  • https://www.naturalearthdata.com was the source for geojson files with political boundaries including countries, states, continents, etc, and physical features such as oceans, lakes, etc.

Geojson editing websites



  • https://geojson.io can create geojson files that include points, lines, and polygons.
  • https://mapshaper.org/ can fix minor errors in geojson files and also reduce file size

Satellite imagery



The Copernicus Sentinel-2 mission provides the satellite data. According to the website, "the revisit frequency of each single SENTINEL-2 satellite is 10 days and the combined constellation revisit is 5 days."
